Aim

To Simulate virtually and Interpret Frequency Modulation and Demodulation waveforms and also to demonstrate how the signal is modulated as the parameters of carrier signal is varied.

Theory


INTRODUCTION
If the frequency of the carrier signal is modulated in accordance with the instantaneous amplitude of the message signal, then this process is called Frequency Modulation (FM). The power level of frequency modulation remains constant because there is no change in the amplitude of the modulated signal.

CONCEPT
The concept of Frequency Modulation can be explained as follows:
At the center frequency of the output signal, the modulating signal will be zero. As the message signal elevates to a positive peak, the modulated signal will be the sum of the center frequency and modulating frequency. Similarly, as the message signal reaches the negative peak, the output waveform will have a frequency which is the difference between the center frequency and modulating frequency. Therefore, the FM wave will deviate between +Δf and −Δf, which is called frequency deviation.
The modulation index of FM is denoted as the ratio of frequency deviation (Δf) to the modulating frequency (fm).

Procedure

  1. Click the "Simulation" tab.

Observations

1. As the amplitude or frequency of the message signal is increased, the modulation pattern becomes more dense or sparse.
2. The carrier waveform remains constant in amplitude and frequency until modulation is applied.
3. The frequency of the modulated waveform changes based on the message signal’s instantaneous amplitude.
4. The demodulated signal waveform approximates the original message signal.
5. A higher message amplitude results in a larger deviation in frequency of the FM signal.

Result & Discussion

The simulation of Frequency Modulation (FM) and Demodulation demonstrates that in FM, the frequency of the carrier signal varies with the amplitude of the message signal, while the amplitude remains constant. The modulated waveform exhibits compressed and expanded cycles depending on the message signal's amplitude, and the demodulated output closely resembles the original message signal.

Precautions

1. Ensure message signal frequency is much lower than the carrier frequency.
2. Avoid setting modulation index too high or too low to prevent distortion.
3. Use correct carrier parameters during demodulation to match modulation settings.
4. Change signal parameters only when the simulation is stopped.
5. Reset the simulation between runs to avoid display or logic errors.
6. Carefully observe waveform changes with each parameter variation.
7. Verify all inputs are within allowed range for amplitude and frequency sliders.
